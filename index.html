Philosophy and Goals

Statement of Philosophy

I am committed to inclusive education by creating a responsive environment where every child's unique developmental needs and cultural backgrounds are valued. I believe that a truly inclusive space is built on the foundation of equitable access, where materials are intentionally selected to meet each child at their current stage of development.

For instance, during sensory-based activities, I provide a diverse range of adaptive tools such as varying sizes of grasps, textured brushes, and height-adjustable surfaces to ensure that the curriculum supports all levels of physical and fine motor development. I ensure that all children, regardless of their individual abilities, can fully participate, exercise their agency, and feel a deep sense of belonging.

Work with Children and Families

Spaghetti and Meat/Veggie Balls

Dramatic Play Sensory Preschool

Summary: In this dramatic play experience, children used textured materials - such as yarn for spaghetti and pompom for "meat/veggie" balls - to simulate a cooking environment. I provided various kitchen tools (tongs, strainers, and pots) to encourage role-playing and collaborative storytelling.

Pedagogical Value: This activity supports symbolic thinking and fine motor coordination as children manipulate different textures and tools. It also promotes social-emotional development through cooperative play, as children negotiate roles and share cultural perspectives on food and family traditions.

Mixing Colours with Eye Droppers

Science Inquiry Preschool

Summary: Using primary-colored water and eye droppers, children explored the scientific process of color transformation. I facilitated this inquiry-based activity by asking open-ended questions, allowing children to predict and observe what happens when colors merge in transparent trays.

Pedagogical Value: This exploration focuses on cognitive development through the understanding of cause and effect. Additionally, the repetitive use of eye droppers strengthens the pincer grasp and hand-eye coordination, essential for early writing and literacy.

Summary:

This framework (ELECT) outlines the six principles of early learning and provides a comprehensive guide to children's development across social, emotional, communication, cognitive, and physical domains.

Significance:

I utilized the ELECT developmental continuum during my placement at CICC to accurately observe children's milestones and plan inclusive activities that support their individual growth stages.

Summary:

This pedagogical document introduces the four foundations of learning - Belonging, Well-being, Engagement, and Expression - to guide program development and professional practice in Ontario.

Significance:

This resource informed my approach to building responsive relationships and creating an environment where every child feels they belong, particularly during my musical engagements and sensory play sessions.
